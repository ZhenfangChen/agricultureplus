  1. Wetlands

    Prior to 1850

    Prior to the Gold Rush of the mid-1800s, the Central Valley of California contained more than 4 million acres of wetlands and supported over 20 million waterfowl during fall and winter.

  2. Emergence of Chinese Labors

    1850

    During the California Gold Rush, the labor of Chinese immigrants was exploited by the mining and agricultural concerns. Farmers who failed in their quest to find a fortune in gold soon learned that there were opportunities to make a living doing what they did back home â€” growing crops to feed a growing population.

  3. The Labor Shortage

    1884

    The Chinese exclusion law of 1884 created a labor shortage, especially for agriculture. Japanese immigrants were brought in to fill the labor gap.

  4. Farming Industry

    1886

    Farming became a large-scale industry. The U.S. began importing Asian labor as African Americans moved into other industries and as the need for labor increased. By 1886, 7 out of every 8 farm workers were Chinese.

  5. The Beginning of Commercial Rice

    1911

    Subsequently, rice was grown successfully in two different areas in the Sacramento Valley in 1911. These plantings marked the beginning of the commercial rice production in California

  6. Flooding

    1919

    1919 heavy rain brought heavy flooding and million dollar damages to many of the Japanese rice-farming pioneers, they lost their investments and life savings in this disaster.

  7. Rice Farming Technology Improvement

    1930

    Start from 1930s, rice farming has witnessed a great improvement in technology. The rice agriculture became more mechanized.Growers began using combine harvesters for swathed rice, followed by new types of techniques including chemical fertilizers, aerial seeding.Since 1950s, most combines were self-propelled and operated by a single person.

  8. Rice Dryer

    1950

    By 1950, most rice was direct combined and dried with a heated-air dryer.

  9. Water-use Efficiency Technology

    1970

    During the 1970s, laser-guided leveling was introduced, resulting in the change from contour levees to parallel levees, improving water-use efficiency. Problems with offsite movement of herbicides from rice fields into the Sacramento River prompted the industry to develop practices to manage herbicide residues in rice field drain waters.

  10. Pollution Control

    1990

    During the 1990s, recirculating and static-water irrigation systems were introduced to further reduce off-site pesticide movement. Growers have become experts at holding water and eliminating drainage after a pesticide application. Air quality concerns led to the introduction of state laws restricting straw burning. As a result, growers started incorporating straw residues in the fall after harvest, followed by flooding of fields during the winter to encourage straw decomposition.

  11. Labor Shortage

    Present

    As African Americans moved to other industries, there was a shortage of labor in the fields. Immigrants, primarily from Latin America, began to work in the fields.
